Accessibility Statement

Last updated May 13, 2026

This statement applies to the https://www.pancakesandbooze.com website.

Our Commitment

MoneyCakes, LLC dba The Pancakes & Booze Art Show is committed to ensuring our services are accessible to people with disabilities. We aim to conform with the WCAG 2.2 Level AA. This accessibility statement forms an integral part of our Terms and Conditions: https://pancakesandbooze.com/terms.

Accessibility Features

You should be able to:

  • Zoom in up to 200% without loss of information or functionality
  • Navigate the website with just a keyboard
  • Listen to the website using a screen reader
  • Skip-to-content link for keyboard users
  • Respects prefers-reduced-motion to pause animations
  • Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements

Conformance Status

Status: Partially compliant with WCAG 2.2 Level AA

We conducted our most recent accessibility evaluation on May 12, 2026. For more information, see the How We Test and Non-Accessible Content sections below.

Non-Accessible Content

Below is a description of known limitations we’re working to address.

  1. Third-party embedded content: Ticket purchase pages and consent management interfaces are hosted by third-party providers (Eventbrite, Etix, Termly) whose accessibility we do not directly control.
  2. Dynamic content from shows.json: City pages and show listings are generated dynamically. While the page templates are accessible, individual show entries may occasionally contain content that does not fully meet accessibility standards.
  3. Marquee animations: The scrolling announcement bar and city ticker pause on mouse hover and respect users’ “reduce motion” preference, but do not pause on keyboard focus.
  4. FAQ keyboard navigation: FAQ category tabs are usable with mouse, touch, and Tab/Enter keys, but do not yet support arrow-key navigation between tabs (full ARIA Tabs pattern).

We are actively working to address these issues as part of an ongoing plan to improve accessibility.

Preparation of This Statement

This statement was prepared on 13 May 2026 and is reviewed annually or when we make significant service changes.

We maintain records of accessibility feedback and our responses for regulatory compliance.

How We Test

We evaluate accessibility using:

  • External accessibility audit
  • Self-assessment conducted in accordance with EAA requirements
  • Automated testing via Lighthouse and axe DevTools
  • Manual keyboard navigation testing
